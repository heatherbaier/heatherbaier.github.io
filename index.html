--- permalink: / title: "About" excerpt: "About me" author_profile: true redirect_from: - /about/ - /about.html --- I am an Assistant Professor at [Arizona State University's School of Geographical Sciences and Urban Planning (SGSUP)](https://geography.asu.edu/). My research sits at the intersection of GeoAI, satellite imagery, and spatial social science — with a focus on how machine learning models can be built, evaluated, and deployed responsibly for socioeconomic and international development applications. My work spans several interconnected themes: predicting socioeconomic outcomes from remotely sensed data, understanding the downstream consequences of coordinate uncertainty and geomasking in GeoAI models, and improving education data systems (EMIS) in the Global South. I am particularly interested in how spatial data quality issues create inequitable prediction errors — and what we can do about it.
