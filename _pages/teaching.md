## Arizona State University

### GIS 521: GIS Programming *(Graduate)*
*School of Geographical Sciences and Urban Planning*

An applied graduate course covering Python-based GIS programming, including spatial data manipulation, remote sensing workflows, and building reproducible geospatial pipelines. Topics include GeoPandas, Rasterio, Google Earth Engine, and spatial analysis with scikit-learn. Students complete a final project applying these skills to a problem of their choosing.

**Topics covered:**
- Python for spatial data (GeoPandas, Shapely, Fiona)
- Raster analysis with Rasterio and GEE
- Urban heat island analysis (replicating Sidiqui et al., 2022, applied to Phoenix)
- Geographically Weighted Regression (GWR)
- Introduction to GeoAI

### GeoAI I *(Graduate, in development)*
*School of Geographical Sciences and Urban Planning*

A new graduate seminar introducing deep learning methods for geospatial applications. Topics span convolutional neural networks for satellite imagery, graph neural networks for spatial data, and the ethical dimensions of deploying GeoAI models in socioeconomic contexts.

### Research Unchained *(Graduate Seminar, proposed)*

A one-semester AI literacy and research skills seminar structured around producing a real peer-reviewed paper with a small interdisciplinary cohort. Students move from literature review to submission over the course of a single semester, with AI tools used intentionally throughout the research process.

## Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on learning by doing. I believe students gain the deepest understanding of computational methods when they apply them to real problems — ideally problems that matter to them or to their communities. I integrate reproducible research practices, open data, and equity considerations into all of my courses.
